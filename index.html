Eligibility & Requirements

Common criteria across the 42 listed scholarships. Individual programs may have additional requirements.

Academic Excellence
Equivalent of First Class Honours or Distinction. Typically GPA 3.7+/4.0, or a 2:1 degree minimum (UK scale). Some programs specify a distinction-level Master's.
Research Experience
Prior research involvement — lab work, thesis, Research Assistantship, or independent projects in AI, ML, or Robotics. Publications at workshop level significantly strengthen applications.
Standardised Tests
GRE 320+ (Quant + Verbal) required for US programs such as Stanford and MIT. European and Asian programs typically do not require the GRE. IELTS 7.0 / TOEFL 100+ for non-native English speakers.
Application Documents
Statement of Purpose (SoP), CV/résumé, official transcripts, 2–3 reference letters (academic/supervisory), and a research proposal. Some programs require a writing sample or portfolio.
International Applicants
The majority of the 42 listed scholarships explicitly welcome applications from international students, including Indian nationals. Some programs offer nationality-blind merit selection; others reserve specific international quotas. Cluster A–B programs are generally open globally; Cluster C–D may have country-specific tracks.

Application Process

A general 6-step framework for applying to the scholarships in this tracker. Use the checklist to track your own readiness.

01
Identify Target Programs
Use the region and focus-area filters in this tracker to shortlist 4–6 programs aligned with your research interest and academic profile. Note deadline clusters.
02
Contact Potential Supervisors
Reach out to faculty whose research aligns with yours 3–6 months before the deadline. A positive reply from a supervisor significantly boosts acceptance rates at most institutions.
03
Prepare Your Documents
Draft a compelling Statement of Purpose (SoP), update your CV, gather official transcripts, and request 2–3 academic reference letters at least 6 weeks before the deadline.
04
Submit Applications
Apply through each institution's official portal. Most programs open applications 2–3 months before the deadline. Complete all sections and upload required attachments in the accepted formats.
05
Interview & Selection
Shortlisted candidates are typically interviewed by a panel of faculty 4–8 weeks after the application deadline. Prepare to discuss your research proposal and academic background clearly.
06
Offer, Visa & Enrolment
Upon receiving an offer, initiate the student visa process for the host country immediately. Processing times range from 4 to 12 weeks depending on nationality and destination.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from international and Indian applicants about the scholarships in this tracker.

Are these scholarships open to Indian nationals? ▾
Yes. All 10 listed programs explicitly welcome applications from international students, including Indian nationals. Selection is merit-based. Some programs have explicit international student allocations; others are entirely nationality-blind. You may need to provide additional documents such as degree equivalency certificates or attested transcripts depending on the institution.
How are stipends converted to ₹ INR on this dashboard? ▾
All ₹ INR values use a consistent indicative conversion rate of 1 RM ≈ ₹19.1, derived from stipend benchmarking against official scholarship data. Local stipends are first converted to RM, then to INR. For example, ETH Zurich's CHF 6,600 monthly stipend ≈ RM 34,000 ≈ ₹6,50,000. These figures are for planning purposes only — always verify current exchange rates via RBI or a licensed currency provider before making financial decisions.
Do I need a publication to apply for these scholarships? ▾
Not always, but publications significantly strengthen applications. For ETH Zurich, MIT Media Lab, and Stanford Knight-Hennessy, a strong research output (even a workshop paper at NeurIPS, ICML, or ICLR) is expected at a competitive level. For AISG, HKPFS, and the Vanier CGS, a compelling research narrative with strong faculty endorsement can compensate. Targeting at least one workshop paper by the time of application significantly strengthens your candidacy.
Can I apply to multiple scholarships at the same time? ▾
Generally yes. Most PhD fellowships do not prohibit simultaneous applications to other programs. Applications are processed independently. However, accepting an offer typically means you must withdraw from other active applications. Check each program's terms and conditions, as a small number may require you to disclose parallel applications.
What GPA or grade is typically required? ▾
Most programs expect the equivalent of a First Class degree. In GPA terms this is typically 3.7+/4.0 or 8.5+/10.0 under Indian grading scales. UK programs use "First Class" or "2:1" as the threshold. The AISG and HKPFS specify "Distinction" or "1st Class." If your official transcript uses a percentage system, include a conversion note or contact the admissions office for guidance.
How often is this tracker updated? ▾
This dashboard is reviewed and updated periodically against official scholarship announcements. Stipend values and deadlines for academic-year cycles may change annually. The exchange rate is indicative. Always cross-reference with the awarding institution's official website before submitting an application or making financial plans.
